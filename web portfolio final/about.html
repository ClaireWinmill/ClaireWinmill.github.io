My Interactive Media Design Experience

My experience working in Interactive Media began in 2014, upon enrolling in, and eventually obtaining a certificate in the Design Studies program at Algonquin College. In this program I developed creativity, learned design principles, and enhanced my drawing skills. Several disciplines were studied, including layout, typography, mixed media, photography, drawing, 3D modelling, and industry standard design software.

Recently, I have nearly completed my first year in the IMD program at Algonquin College, I have explored the primary elements of media project planning, and practiced hands-on media production through courses in computer-based web design and programming as well as video and motion graphics.

My Visual & Fine Art Experience

After completing Design Studies at Algonquin College, I enrolled in a 3-year, Fine Arts program at Ottawa School of Art, and obtained a Fine Arts diploma. This was an intensive three-year visual arts program with an emphasis on practical studio experience. The developmental structure of the program provided me with access to various studio disciplines including; drawing, painting, sculpture, photography, printmaking and ceramics, while including sufficient opportunity to pursue a particular studio concentration. Although the focus of this program was fine art, I gained a strong understanding of design concepts including; composition, colour theory, and visual harmony that now help in my Interactive Media Designs.
